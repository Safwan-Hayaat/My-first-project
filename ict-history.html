Information and Communication Technology and its history

Syed Abdul Mustayan and Safwan Hayaat

ICT is used to trade at the world stock exchange
ICT (Information and Communication Technology) is a broad term that covers many different things, such as Electronic Communication, Television, Computer Network and Satellite Systems and beyond.

ICT is an extremely important part of today’s world, being used for Medicine, Entertainment, Communication and just life in general. Infact, the governments of the world also use ICT for many different reasons like running defense systems, organizing finances and managing different organizations etc.

Because of how broad ICT is, many people who were born centuries before ICT was even a word can be listed as pioneers of ICT. For example, Al-Khwarizmi, called “Algorismus” by Europeans

Muhammad Ibn Musa Al-Khwarizmi

محمد بن موسى الخوارزميّ

Kitab Al-Jabor
Al-Khwarizmi has made many contributions in all subjects. But his most important work might be inventing many algebraic equations. For example, his book in the 9th century called “Al-Kitab Al-Muktasir Fi Hisab Al-Jabor Wal-Muqobbala“. The book is called “Al-Jabor” in short. Which is the namesake for Algebra.

But he also made major contributions to ICT indirectly. He made many books on arithmetic and algorithms, especially on Indian arithmetic. One of his many works includes “Kitab Al-Hisab Al-Hindi” which was a deep dive into algorithms on basic decimals.

Eventually, his works made their way into Europe, where these works were legendary. It improved their understanding of algorithms to the point that Khwarizmi’s texts were used for centuries onwards. When computers were invented, an important component of them was algorithms. Without Khwarizmi’s works, perhaps we might not have had computers today. And even if we did, they would not have been as technologically advanced as they are today.

As mentioned before, he was called “Algorismus” by Europeans, which is the namesake for the word "Algorithm". It’s hard to imagine a person who was so ahead of his time that even a thousand years later, his works are still extremely important. It’s impossible to put into words just how intelligent he was.

He truly was one of the greatest mathematicians of his era.

Alan Mathison Turing

A picture of the Bombe
Alan Turing was a British Cryptographer (a cryptographer is one who studies ciphers and codes). He, like Al-Khwarizmi, made major contributions to the invention of the computer. He is most known for cracking the “impossible to break cipher”, the Enigma.

The Enigma was a cipher used by the Germans during World War II, which was a code that would randomize each letter of the alphabet into another letter, and it randomized everyday. Because of this, the enigma was used to hide secret messages. It had over 159 Quintillion (158,962,555,217,826,360,000) possible variations. Which is why the code was considered impossible to break.

But, in 1939 and 1940, Alan Turing, with the help of Polish cryptographers, made a machine called the “Bombe” it worked by calculating one possible combination after another and eliminating each combination by contradiction until only a couple of combinations were left. By that point the possible combinations were manually checked, until the actual message was revealed.

Today, he is credited for ending World War II early and saving the lives of millions of people.

Despite all of this, his greatest gift to ICT may have been the Turing Machine, a theoretical model that explained how a machine can process information step by step. This idea is still used today to understand algorithms, programming, and problem-solving in ICT. Even though Charles Babbage is credited with inventing the Computer, without this concept, modern computers or even software and smartphones would not exist in the way we know them.

Alan Turing also made huge progress in artificial intelligence. He introduced the famous Turing Test, which is used to check whether a machine can think like a human. This test is still discussed today in AI research, robotics, and machine learning. Because of his ideas, we now have virtual assistants, smart algorithms, and AI systems that can learn and make decisions. His thinking was far ahead of his time, and many people did not understand him when he was alive. Alan Turing was a pioneer, a war hero, the creator of computer theory, and an inspiration for modern technology.

Jagadish Chandra Bose

জগদীশ চন্দ্র বসু

A picture of the Crescograph
Jagadish Chandra Bose was a famous scientist from our subcontinent. He made some of the most important contributions to the world through his inventions and scientific discoveries, even though he never got the recognition he truly deserved in his lifetime. He was a pioneer in wireless communication, and his experiments with radio waves happened many years before other famous scientists. Bose successfully showed how electromagnetic waves could be transmitted without wires, which later helped in the development of the radio, television, Wi-Fi and even the mobile communication that we use every single day now.

Another very big contribution of Jagadish Chandra Bose was in plant science. He invented special instruments like the Crescograph, which could measure extremely tiny movements in plants. With this, he proved that plants are alive and react to pain, heat, cold and touch, which shocked many people at that time. His research connected physics and biology in a way no one really tried before. Bose also believed knowledge should be free, so he did not patent many of his inventions and shared them with the world. Because of this, science grew faster. Overall, Jagadish Chandra Bose changed modern science forever and his contributions still affect our world to this day.
In conclusion, we can tell that ICT is something that is incredibly broad. Going over different millennia and different subjects. from an ancient mathematician to a British cryptologist. Seemingly random people in history have somehow or in some way contributed to ICT as we know it today. Nowadays, we take all of this for granted. Not realizing the centuries of innovation and the brilliance that went into creating the Computers, Televisions and the Communication technology we have today. So, we should all be thankful for the hardwork of these genius minds that aided us in the creation of the technology we have today.
